What are Vocational Courses? Scope Career Opurtunitiess


Vocational courses are an educational discipline that enables individual to acquire skills that are required for a particular trade.
Vocational courses are traditionally non academic and are completely related to a specific trade, occupation or vocation.


These courses are designed to impart application based study where theoretical aspects are not studied independently.
Students after 10th standard can now opt for short and long term courses like diplomas and degrees. There are variety of courses available for students to choose from.
Vocational courses has diversified itself providing a wider scope for students after 10th standard.
Vocational Courses are offered by various institutes, colleges, schools and government aided institutions.

Vocational Course Topics


Vocational courses are offered in various fields including health care, creative fields like graphic and web designing, food technology and cosmetology.
Vocational courses are also provided in skilled trades like automotive repair, plumbing, heating and air conditioning.

Popular vocational courses are as follows:


Hotel Management


This course needs the passion to cook and serve, those students with such passion might opt for hospitality management field. Various colleges offers diploma in hotel management with specialisations.

Diploma in graphic designing/web designing


Students with creativity and love for animation can do well in Graphic designing. This course is offered at various private institutes.

Acting for film and television


Students with interest and passion for acting and drama can take up diploma courses in art of film making. Institutes like Asian Academy of Film and Television offer diploma course in Acting for film and television.

Benefits of studying vocational courses


  • Vocational course provides knowledge of the subjects as well as vocational skills.
  • Vocational courses focus on specialised practical skills.
  • These courses offer insight to requirements of particular types of jobs.
  • Vocational courses provide specialised learning which is an added advantage.
  • There are diversified courses to provide skilled manpower in the organised sector.
